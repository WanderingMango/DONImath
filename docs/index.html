The Unintended Consequence

Congress accidentally created a financial incentive to reclaim your birth name. The math is below.

$0. Filing fee if you qualify for a fee waiver.

Fee waivers are available to anyone receiving public benefits or below your state's income threshold. If you're a stay-at-home spouse with no personal income, you almost certainly qualify.

Changing your name requires a court petition. Filing fees range from $0 to $450 depending on your personal income, which means changing your name back to your birth name may be cheaper than certifying all the documents required to vote.

When it's done, your birth certificate and your driver's license match. You never have to do this again.
